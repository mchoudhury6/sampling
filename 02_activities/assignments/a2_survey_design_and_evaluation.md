## Part B - Survey Evaluation:

Identify and describe survey features:

```
1. **Sample Type**  
The survey employed stratified sampling based on province/CMA and randomly selected household members aged 15 and older.

2. **Sample Size**  
The survey sample included 50,000 units, with 40,000 invitation letters sent for online participation.

3. **Target Population**  
The target population consisted of individuals aged 15 and above living in private households across Canada, excluding residents of Yukon, Northwest Territories, Nunavut, and full-time institutional residents.

4. **Sampling Frame**  
The sampling frame was created by combining telephone numbers (landline & mobile) from Census and administrative sources with Statistics Canada's dwelling frame.

5. **Survey Mode(s)**  
Data was collected through telephone interviews and, for the first time in 2018, via an online questionnaire.

6. **Timeline**  
Data collection took place between September 4, 2018, and December 28, 2018.

7. **Response Rate**  
The survey aimed to receive 24,000 responses from the 50,000 invitations sent.

8. **Weights**  
The survey included WGHT_PER for individual estimates, bootstrap weights for variance, and adjustments to ensure representativeness.

9. **Data Processing**  
A structured processing environment (SSPE) was used to ensure high-quality data. Automated and manual edits were performed at both macro and micro levels.

10. **Cleaning, Imputation, etc.**  
Survey records underwent computer edits, including built-in questionnaire checks, family relationship checks, consistency checks, and flow edits to ensure valid responses. Missing data was imputed in a 9-step process, including imputing personal and family income, volunteering variables, and donation-related data. 81.9% of personal income and 81.7% of family income were linked to tax data.

11. **Sources of Error**  
Errors included typographical mistakes, editing assumptions, and respondent errors such as misinterpretation, memory lapses, or omissions.

12. **Limitations, Known Biases, etc.**  
Non-sampling errors arose from underrepresented groups due to non-responses or omitted answers. There was selection bias due to the exclusion of non-phone users, as the survey could only be completed via telephone or online. Income-related analysis could be impacted by data linkage limitations, as some respondents opted not to link their tax data.

13. **Link to Documentation and Additional Sources**  
[Documentation Link](https://www150.statcan.gc.ca/n1/pub/45-25-0001/index-eng.htm)
```
